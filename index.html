Personal Profile

I believe everyone has, what I call, an "Astronaut Dream." Or maybe very few of us do and I'm in the minority, but that's close to what I wanted. When I began my college career four years ago, I initially had plans to become a movie director. It's so far from where I am now that it makes me smile when I think of how wrong I was on my first choice. Intentionally, I always left technology as a back-up. What I should have realized from the beginning was my passion for technology is my greatest strength.

Some people may say things weren't always that way or maybe they had a special turning point in their lives, but for me technology has always been my passion, I just needed to accept that. I've been toying with computers and their subsiduaries my entire life. My destiny was set when my father allowed me to take over an old Dell Inspiron he had received from work. To friends and family, it was always between my dad and I when there was a computer problem. To them, I became their personal IT guy, but to me, the sky became my limit. I've since pursued other avenues in my life, all the while relating to technology in one way or another.

I like to spend most of my free time with friends, participating in various activities, movies or events around the city. I'm an avid musician as well and attempt to learn as much as I can tolerate of an instrument before I end up overloading myself. In addition, I'll spend time developing side projects to further my knowledge, talents and abilities. Projects such as this website.
